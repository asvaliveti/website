The $2.5 trillion fashion industry is one of the biggest polluters and the second-biggest consumer of water.The major issue is that most of the fabrics in cheap garments are synthetics and polyesters, which are derived from oil and petroleum production.Unlike wool or cotton, synthetic particles don’t biodegrade. So when clothes are dumped into a landfill, toxic synthetic fibers pollute water sources.
